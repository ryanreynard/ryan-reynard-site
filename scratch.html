Hobby No. 1

Fly Fishing

There's nothing like standing knee-deep in a cold river at dawn, watching a dry fly drift over a riffle and disappear in a sudden swirl. Fly fishing isn't just a hobby — it's a discipline. Reading water, matching the hatch, presenting the fly without spooking the fish. Every outing is a puzzle worth solving.

I've fished rivers across Utah and the intermountain west, mostly chasing brown trout and cutthroats. Below is a breakdown of some of my favorite rivers and the flies that work on each.

Favorite Rivers & Flies

  1. Provo River — Utah's premier tailwater
    • Zebra Midge (#18–22)
    • RS2 (olive, #20)
    • Parachute Adams (#16)
  2. Green River — A section below Flaming Gorge
    • San Juan Worm (red, #14)
    • Pheasant Tail Nymph (#16–18)
    • Elk Hair Caddis (#14)
  3. Logan River — Small stream canyon fishing
    • Stimulator (#12)
    • Copper John (#16)
    • Woolly Bugger (black, #8)
  4. Henry's Fork (Idaho) — Technical dry fly water
    • Pale Morning Dun (#18)
    • CDC Sparkle Dun (#18)
    • Blue-Winged Olive (#20)

Hobby No. 2

Powerlifting

Powerlifting is simple in theory and brutal in practice: squat, bench press, deadlift. Go as heavy as possible. Do it safely. Repeat. What keeps me coming back is the honesty of the barbell — it doesn't care about your excuses, only your effort.

Training has taught me patience, consistency, and how to push through when everything in you wants to stop. Skills that transfer directly to business, leadership, and life.
